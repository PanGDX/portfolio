### TLDR;

- Analyzes different taxation models through the lens of John Rawls' "Justice as Fairness".
- Discusses the ethical implications of progressive, regressive, and flat tax systems.
- Argues that a just tax system should align with the Difference Principle, benefiting the least advantaged members of society.
- Concludes that a progressive tax system is most consistent with Rawlsian principles.
